Japan & Racism Workshop

In August 2016 I had the honor to conduct a workshop at the Idrætshøjskole Bosei situated in Præstø, southern Sjælland. As part of the IFAS program, 14 Japanese exchange students got hands-on experiences of the concept of a Danish Folkehøjskole.

I designed and customized a workshop about racism in Japan and ways to fight it in everyday life.

Part 1: “What is racism?”
Part 2: “How can we speak up against racism?”

In my two-part workshop (2x 90 Min), I implemented a broad set of methods ranging from discussions, over a poster making session to a theatre sketch with a racist situation (solution finding). I conducted the workshop in English and had 2 Japanese translators by my side whenever communication was crucial.

As a main part of the workshop, we watched the movie Hafu, which draws a very intimate picture of what racial discriminations looks like in present-day Japan.

The most challenging part of the workshop was the actual implementation of our knowledge in everyday life. The participants had a lot of freedom designing their posters and theatre sketch. At the end, we engaged in a concluding discussion about practicability and obstacles of our ideas and strategies.

Creating these workshops enrichened my perspective a lot and gave me a good skillset of cultural sensitivity across language barriers.

At the end of the day, I facilitated a teambuilding workshop together with Danish participants in order to strengthen the bonds of the participants and end our day on a positive and fun note.
